Augment Theory
Econ Augments
These augments are recommended at stage 2-1 as it allows you to reach level 8 by stage 4-2 on schedule with enough gold (about 40) to roll for upgraded board These are less good later stages (besides fast 9 meta) however can be taken in emergencies if you are very poor and very low on HP and you want to try get a top 6

Examples:

Silver : Missed connections

Gold : Clear/Cluttered mind, Patient study

Prismatic : Hedgefund, Level up
Item Augments
These augments are recommended at stage 2-1 and 3-2 as it allows you to have early board strength and go on win streaks, also have strong mid game with more itemized units. These augments are not great at stage 4 as combat augments outscale them.

Examples :

Silver : Buried treasures, item grab bag

Gold : Buried treasures, Support augments, Portable forge

Prismatic : Overwhelming force, Impenetrable Bulwark
Combat Augments
These augments are recommended at stage 3 and 4 as they provide good late game scaling once you have made it to the late game. They can be taken at stage 2 but more often than not you will lose streak early game.

Examples :

Silver : Vampirism

Gold : Gift of the fallen, Jeweled lotus, Combat caster

Prismatic : Jeweled lotus, Harmacist
Tailored Augments
These augments force you into playing a specific comp, they are terrible for stage 2 but okay at stage 4 when you are certain you know what you are playing.

Examples :

Gold : Trickshot or Porcelain Augments
Augment Theory
This is the order of augment choice to follow to improve your games. (Obviously OP augments may change the overall theory)

Econ > Combat > Combat (Playing comps with 1 main carry)

Econ > Item > Combat (Standard)

Item > Item > Combat (EASY TOP 4 IF WIN STREAKING)

Item > Combat > Combat (Strong early game and scaling)

Combat > Item > Combat (Stage 3-2 winout)

Combat > Combat > Combat (LOSE STREAK WINOUT)
